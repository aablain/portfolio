Who I Am

  1. Clark Community College
  2. Western Oregon University
  3. Youth With a Mission:DTS
  4. Epicodus (in process)

My name is Austin, and I love being creative. After High School, I Obtained my Associates Degree at Clark Community College in Vancouver WA. While enrolled at Clark, I took a class for HTML/CSS that opened my eyes to the coding world. Computers had always interested me but I never knew it was something I could actually do. However, after Clark I transfered to Western Oregon University to study Music Performance (specifically in Percussion and Drumset). After WOU, I attended a "Discipleship Training School" in an Orginization called YWAM, which stands for Youth With a Mission. My school in YWAM brought me to study for a fall in Maui, as well as 3 monthes of hands-on training all over the nation of Nepal. I am now attending Epicodus in Portland, Oregon in order that I might learn to code at a more professional level. I've always enjoyed creating in just about any medium. I believe I can use my creativity in the realm of coding to add something worthwhile- and if nothing else, bring a higher quality of life to myself and my family. I plan on learning Ruby on Rails, Javascript, and much much more!

Apart from coding I have many hobbies and passions in my life. Probably the biggest in my life would be music and specifically drumming (if attending school for music wasn't evidence enough). I enjoy learning about music of all genres and how the drums are used in each. I play drumset for many churches in the Portland/Vancouver Metro area, as well as partake in a few music projects- some of which are on the drums, and some as pianist/lyricist. Like many native Pacific Northwesterners, I love to hike and explore the outdoors. In fact, the house I grew up in my entire life sits inside a forest in Clark County, Washington. Some of my fondest childhood memories took place in that forest; whacking back brush, finding trails, inspecting giant mushrooms that cling to trees, and almost always coming back home dirty with scraped up legs.

I value Honesty and Integrity. I'm human so I'm not perfect- I make mistakes. I do my best to own up to my mistakes and shortcomings. I think that living a life of honor doesn't mean perfection, it means humility and truthfulness. I desire to live an honorable life.
